Introduction/Overview

  • Nuclear energy contributes ~19% of U.S. electricity and is the largest source of carbon-free power.
  • The U.S. aims to triple nuclear capacity by 2050 (DOE).
  • Although nuclear has high upfront costs, it offers low long-term operating costs (IEA).
  • Emerging trends include Small Modular Reactors (SMRs) and fusion.
  • Key challenges include waste disposal, safety regulations, and an aging reactor fleet.
